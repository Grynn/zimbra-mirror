Adding your signature automatically

You can create signatures for your email messages. Your signature can include your name and additional closing text to the maximum number of characters that your account allows.

For example, your signature could read:

John Smith
Vice President of Engineering
Widgets Division
Acme Corporation, Inc.
303-555-1212 x111
john.smith@example.com

If you create multiple identities, you can create multiple signatures and assign them to different addresses. See Managing your account identities.

To create a signature:

  1. Open Preferences and select the Signatures tab.

  2. Click Edit Signature.

  3. In the Signature Name field give your signature and identifiable name.  This is not your signature, but an identifiable heading. For example, you could call the signature "Formal" to describe that it includes your name, title, company name, etc.

  4. In the Signature text box, type the signature information exactly as you want it to appear in your messages.

  5. Click Save.

  6. Go to the Accounts tab to select which signature to display in your messages by default.

  7. Click Save.
